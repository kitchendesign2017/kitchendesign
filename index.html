Notes:

This is a proposed initial kitchen layout. Details are still subject to change, but this is an attempt to verify that appliances will fit. Also, I didn't spend a lot of effort picking perfectly designed 3D models of all cabinets. Don't pay attention to finishes at this point.

All countertops are going to be a little higher than standard, in order to make it easier for us to work on them without bending down. For now, I am going with 38", but we can finalize details later.

Instead of 24", countertops are going to be 30" deep. We'll have to either build a custom shelve behind the range, or we'll have to modify it with a Bluestar island-trim vent. This probably also means that the upper cabinets will gain an extra 6" in depth.

For the time being, I have drawn the top of the upper cabinets at 8 feet from the floor. That is definitely something we need to still finalize.

The kitchen is intended to be fully functional for everyday use, but it does rely on storage outside of the kitchen for rarely used items (e.g. special purpose small counter-top appliances, fresh towels, bulky pantry items, ...).

While we have gotten initial rough pricing information, we'll have to go back and get exact pricing, when we have a better idea of the final appliance choices. At that point, we might have to make additional revisions based on pricing or availability.

In order to maximize fridge/freezer space, we are going to use a Sub-Zero 36" all-fridge, and two Perlick 24" under-counter freezer drawers. This nominally provides 23.3 cu. ft. of refrigerator space, and 10.4 cu. ft. of freezer space. The freezer drawers will be installed in a kitchen island just outside of the actual kitchen. This island extends into the dining table.

We will continue using the existing 30" BlueStar RNB range. We had hoped to upgrade to a Platinum series range that was a little wider. But upon further review, it turns out the primary motivation would be getting an additional small (i.e. fast pre-warm) oven. Achieving that goal requires at least a 48" range, which seems impractical in the available space. The secondary motivation would have been a slightly bigger cook surface with maybe two more burners. But that's not as important. So, instead, we'll stay with what we have and install a dedicated steam wall oven.

We want a plumbed steam oven, which (in the US market) pretty much narrows it down to a Miele 24"Ã—18" combi steam oven.

We also need a solution for a micro-wave, even if we expect that it will be used much more rarely. We picked the most basic Miele model available, in order to easily match with the steam oven. The speed oven feature is a nice bonus, but is not entirely critical for our needs.

No decision has been made on the vent hood yet. We need to narrow down the cabinet design a little more before we can decide on whether we want a liner or a full hood. We probably want a remote blower and an in-line muffler to minimize noise, though. We most likely will need a make-up-air system, too.

We will continue using the existing 24" Miele dishwasher.

We have gotten very used to our trash compactor and will buy the current KitchenAid model, as that's apparently the only option on the market, anyway.

The sink has not been specified in detail, yet. For now, our thinking is that we'll have an asymmetrically divided custom-built farmhouse sink. The divider should be low enough that I can hopefully wash a cookie sheet in the sink without too much trouble. We'll need to research what type of sink grids are available for the larger partition, or if we can forgo that.

The sink will have a soap dispenser that directly plugs into a large supply bottle (i.e. Costco sized).

We should try to find a way to store a pull-out trashcan for recycling underneath the sink.

We'll have a garbage disposal in the smaller partition of the sink. No decision on exact specifications for the garbage disposal, as of yet. But that should be pretty easy to nail down.

There should be a pot filler at the back of the stove.

We looked at a couple of water-filter options, but haven't reached a conclusion at this point. And regretfully, we might not be able to get the water carbonation solution that I had originally been hoping for. It sounds as if Everpure has taken their product off the US market.

An alternative option would be the Grohe water carbonation system. I am still figuring out whether the reviews are mostly positive or mostly negative. It sounds as if a lot of the negative reviews are a result of poor installation, which could skew results.

The layout currently shows about 3 feet of pantry space. That's a nice upgrade over what we have right now. But in practice, we'll probably have to shave some space off of the pantry as we finalize cabinet space. So, consider the big pantry a place-holder and we'll figure out the exact layout later.

Also, there is currently no obviously good location to place towels or pot holders. The wall next to the window is probably the best place to consider for this purpose.

The remaining counter-top appliances at this point are the water kettle and the toaster. While not a high priority, we should brain-storm if these two appliances can be eliminated.
